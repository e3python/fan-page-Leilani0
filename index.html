The "TikTok Ruined This"

Songs that were great until they were played 9 million times in 15-second clips.

The Academic Version:

"Welcome to the official investigation into the TikTok Song Graveyard. In the modern era, a hit song's greatest blessing is also its biggest curse: going viral. While TikTok can turn an underground track into a global anthem overnight, the platform's 15-second loop cycle has a habit of turning masterpieces into psychological weapons. We are exploring the 'Skip Reflex'—that moment when a song has been used in so many transitions, dance challenges, and 'Get Ready With Me' videos that the human brain simply cannot listen to it anymore without experiencing a mental short-circuit. These are the songs we loved, until the FYP wouldn't let us."

"Dreams" by Fleetwood Mac
Once a masterpiece of 70s rock, now forever known as "The Cranberry Juice Song." It's impossible to hear that bassline without imagining someone on a skateboard looking way too relaxed.
"Savage Love" by Jawsh 685 and Jason Derulo
That siren beat was catchy for exactly 14 seconds. By the 15th second (and the 500th video), it became a psychological weapon.
"Blinding Lights" by The Weeknd
A synth-pop legend that was sacrificed so that entire families could do synchronized jumping dances in their living rooms.
"Say So" by Doja Cat
The song that proved a 15-second dance could change the world. It's a great song, but if you hear the intro, your arms might start doing the "Say So" dance automatically. It's a reflex now.
"Renegade" / "Lottery" by K CAMP
The ultimate "Viral Dance" song. It didn't just ruin the song; it created a whole new era of people staring intensely at their front-facing cameras.
"Stunnin'" by Curtis Waters
"Ice on my neck..." We get it. You're stunnin'. This song was the official anthem of every "glow up" transition video for a year straight.
"Supalonely" by BENEE
The official anthem of the 2020 lockdowns. Hearing it now doesn't just remind us of the song; it reminds us of being stuck inside for months. Too soon!
"Roses (Imanbek Remix)" by SAINT JHN
That high-pitched, sped-up vocal was the soundtrack to every "cool car" or "slow-mo" video. It's the sonic equivalent of a neon light—cool at first, then it just gives you a headache.
"WAP" by Cardi B
Regardless of how you feel about the lyrics, the "Split Challenge" on TikTok resulted in way too many people accidentally ending up in the emergency room.
